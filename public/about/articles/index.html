Article 1: Balancing Track and Tech

In this article I talk about how I balance being a student-athlete with learning tech and doing school projects like this website.

Most days I’m juggling classes, practice, lifting, homework, and trying to still have downtime. It’s not perfect, but having both track and tech in my life motivates me and keeps me moving forward.

Article 2: Why Small Projects Matter

Small projects like this website help me learn faster than big, overwhelming assignments.

When I build something small, I can focus on one idea at a time, test it, and fix mistakes without stressing. Most of the things I’ve learned about coding came from little experiments, not huge projects.

This website is another small step toward getting better at web development.

Article 3: My Website Enhancement

For my enhancement, I customized parts of the Ananke theme. I changed the colors, spacing, and some of the visual style so the site felt more like something I designed and not just the default theme.

I explored the theme files, figured out where styles were coming from, and overrode them in my own stylesheet. It took trial and error, but it helped me understand how Hugo themes are structured.

Article 4: Using AI While Building This Website

I used AI mainly as a helper to guide me through Hugo’s structure, Git commands, and the assignment requirements. It helped me fix errors, organize my folders correctly, and write cleaner descriptions for my pages.

AI was helpful for explanations and examples, but I still had to understand the structure myself. Sometimes the answers didn’t fit my setup exactly, so I had to adjust things on my own.

Overall, AI made the process faster, but the actual decisions and final work were still mine.
